About Me

I am an associate researcher (Principal Investigator) at the Institute of Advanced Agricultural Sciences, Peking University. I apply modeling and quantitative methods to study markets with primary focuses on agricultural, food, and resource markets. I obtained my PhD in Agricultural and Resource Economics from University of California, Davis under the guidance of Prof. Richard J. Sexton and Prof. Rachael E. Goodhue.

Research

Yujing Song, Meilin Ma
May 2023 Job Market Paper

Relational Contracts in Well-Functioning Markets: Evidence from China's Vegetable Wholesale

Using detailed transaction-level data of a large wholesale produce market in China, this paper explores the value of relational contracting alongside an active spot market and suggests the strategic complementarity between the two.

PDF Video
Yujing Song, Rachael Goodhue, Leo Simon, Luca Colombo
May 2021 Invited Submission to The Annual Review of Resource Economics

Market Power in Dynamic Models of Natural Resource Markets: Theory, Empirics, and the Lerner Index

We review the theoretical and empirical literatures on market power in dynamic models of markets for renewable and non-renewable natural resources.

Leo Simon, Rachael Goodhue, Yujing Song, Gordon Rausser, Sophie Thoyer, Sylvie Morardet, Patrick Rio
April 2021 Working Paper

Structure and Power in Multilateral Negotiations: An Application to French Water Policy

We apply the Rausser-Simon multilateral bargaining model to a specific negotiation process, involving water storage capacity and use in the upper part of the Adour Basin in south-western France.
